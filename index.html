Understanding Disaster Risks in Nepal

Natural disasters in Nepal occur unexpectedly and can cause severe damage to communities. The country's diverse geography makes it vulnerable to various hazards that threaten lives and property.

Nepal frequently experiences earthquakes due to its location on major fault lines. These seismic events can destroy buildings, infrastructure, and result in significant loss of life.

During monsoon season, heavy rainfall causes flooding in the Terai region and landslides in hilly areas. Floods damage crops, homes, and disrupt transportation networks across affected districts.

Landslides are common in mountainous regions, blocking roads, burying settlements, and cutting off remote communities from essential services and emergency assistance.

The impact of these disasters extends beyond immediate damage, affecting livelihoods, education, and long-term development. Many families lose their homes and sources of income.

Early warning systems and public awareness are crucial for reducing disaster risks. Timely alerts allow communities to prepare, evacuate if necessary, and minimize casualties.

This website provides basic information about disaster risks in Nepal. Understanding these hazards is the first step toward building safer, more resilient communities.

Remember: Preparedness saves lives. Stay informed, have an emergency plan, and know evacuation routes for your area.
